The face behind Terra Bonum

The Bonwire legacy

Where kente was born.

Legend tells of two weavers of Bonwire who watched a spider spin its web and returned home to weave the first kente from raffia. Centuries later, this village in the Ashanti Region remains the beating heart of the craft.

At Terra Bonum Enterprise, every cloth is woven strip by strip on the traditional loom. No two are the same — each colour and pattern carries a proverb, a blessing, a name.

From thread to cloth

The craft, in three hands

01

Spinning & dyeing

Cotton and silk threads are hand-dyed in the deep reds, golds and greens that give kente its voice.

02

Weaving the strips

On the narrow loom, the weaver builds strips just four inches wide — a single cloth can take weeks.

03

Joining & finishing

Strips are hand-sewn edge to edge so the patterns align — then pressed, checked and readied to ship.
